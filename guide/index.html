Welcome to Little Miss Mafia!

This guide is intended for players new to the game of Mafia as a whole, but it might also be helpful to experienced players looking to learn the specifics of this game.

Mafia Explained

Mafia is a social deduction game where players are secretly assigned roles. The Town and Mafia factions aim to eliminate each other, while Neutral players have their own win conditions.

The Town start out in the majority, and can use their numbers to their advantage, voting out suspected Mafia members during the daytime. Meanwhile, the Mafia start out in the minority, but their abilities are powerful and they may work together in secret.

The game alternates between Day and Night phases.

Day Phase

  • Players may talk freely about their suspicions and results. The Town must collaborate to find out who is lying.
  • From the second day, everyone votes for a player to eliminate.
  • Some roles have special day abilities.

Night Phase

  • The main chat is closed, though Mafia may still talk among themselves.
  • Most roles have abilities to use. Investigative roles can gather information for their team, Killing roles can attack, and so on.
  • The Mafia also chooses someone to attack.

Role Types

Factions

The game is primarily divided into three factions: Town, Mafia, and Neutral. Each player is assigned one of 60 possible roles at random. You can learn about each role in detail using the "View Role Details" tab on this page.

Roles are further split into archetypes, which define their general purpose within their team.

Town Roles

Power: Unique, especially strong roles

Investigative: learn information about others

Protective: mitigate damage or other harm

Killing: deal damage to others

Disruptive: hinder other players

Communicative: create special chats

Support: help the Town in various other ways

Mafia Roles

Investigative: learn information about the Town

Protective: keep fellow Mafia members safe

Killing: deal extra damage to the Town

Disruptive: get in the way of the Town

Deceptive: confuse and mislead the Town

Support: help the Mafia in various other ways

Neutral Roles

Benign: generally harmless to the Town

Evil: work against town interests

Killing: powerful killers working against both the Town and the Mafia to be the last one standing
